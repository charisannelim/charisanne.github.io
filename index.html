ABOUT

A Little Bit About Me!

My name is Charis (which means 'grace' in Greek!), and I am a passionate designer and developer from Yale-NUS College who is interested in crafting meaningful user experiences, digital or non-digital. I’ve created content for the government, nationwide movements and various school campaigns.

I adore community arts, local handicrafts and doodles, especially children’s doodles. When I am not working on my next programming assignment, I am either stuffing my face at my grandmother’s house, playing floorball or watching a Korean drama or two. Or three. I am currently employed at GovTech Singapore, but feel free to contact me for a chat over bubble tea to learn more!

Work

What I've dabbled in the past couple of years

Senior Capstone

For my capstone project, I collaborated with Saw Swee Hock School of Public Health to design and evaluate HomeAx, a digital home assessment tool for patients with physical impairments. This tool enables occupational therapists and caregivers to easily assess the patient's surroundings and ensure his or her safety at home. The research paper was accepted by and eventually presented as part of CHI 2019 in Glasgow, Scotland.

Fomo Sumo

For a software development module in NUS (CS3216), my team and I developed a web-based motion-controlled multiplayer game suite, which won first place at the end-of-semester project showcase. I designed the user interface and game assets, and spearheaded the marketing campaign.

SoShare

SoShare is a progressive web application that helps people to easily crowdsource interested parties to share deals with. Within a tight deadline of four weeks, I worked with my team to develop this application. As one of the main designers, I crafted the user interface and experience, and worked on the frontend as well.

Rakkoon

Rakkoon is the one-stop destination to share and know about free food deals - from surplus foods to promotions to many more. I designed this high-fidelity prototype for a mobile application.

Retro 2018

During my semester abroad in Seoul, I worked with a team to produce an installation for an interactive media design module. Making use of LeapMotion technology, Ableton Live, MAX/MSP and Processing, we translated user movements into different images and melodies. I programmed and put together all the visual elements.

Journey with George

As a Special Projects intern at Lien Foundation, a philanthropic organization, I collaborated closely with emerging animator Khoo Siew May to direct and produce a short animation depicting experiences of a child with special needs in Singapore. 'Journey With George' attained over 1,000 shares and 50,000 views. Click to watch the clip!

S-League: Fantasy Football

For this programming module, my team and I built a game from scratch using Java, creating a Singaporean edition of the online game Fantasy Football. I designed and coded the entire graphical user interface for this application. Our team also obtained the highest grade among all the teams for our concept, experience and functionalities. Click to go to our Github repository.

INDIGITOUS' #HACK: #TACKLINGSUICIDE

In response to the question ‘How might we create a nationwide community of allies that are empowered to help young adults suffering from depression and suicidal thoughts?’, my team conceptualized J10YOU, an online platform that educates, encourages and equips allies, eventually clinching the People's Choice Award. I designed the high-fidelity prototype for this web platform during the weekend hackathon.

Bedok Church of Christ

A traditional church does not mean it has to be stuck in the past! As a side project, I volunteered to revamp our church’s website into a modern, sleek site that looks good on all devices.

Yale-NUS Singers' Guild Concert 2017

I was tasked to conceptualize and carry out the creative direction for my school’s annual Singers’ Guild concert: On That Note. The publicity material I designed included posters, program booklet and tickets for the event.
